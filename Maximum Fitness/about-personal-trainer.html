On being a qualified personal trainer

It was a huge boost knowing I was now qualified to put my training to the test with customers, but I still wasn’t completely satisfied. I knew there was more to learn out there.
That’s when I started self-experiments. Month after month I would trial new techniques and tweak some exercises, I didn’t want to be just another personal trainer that helps you get in to shape.
I wanted grunt, power, and conditioning, something that would make my clients stand out from the rest. It brought me back to my initial interest, athletes, sports, and challenges!

SO I developed my program and training techniques whilst using myself as the guinea pig and then passed on the theories to the World’s Number 1 IBF Featherweight Champion, Billy The Kid Dib.
I was always a fan of Billy Dib, he is a born fighter, but like many professional sports athletes there is always an area that can be improved. For Billy, It was his need for greater strength and a killer knockout punch. I wanted to take his body condition to a place it’s never been before. After refining my “Train Like A Pro” program on Billy, I turned it into a professional program that anyone can use to take their strength and fitness to a new level.

Results

Billy Dib’s dramatic conditioning reformation and phenomenal increase in strength and power, played a vital role in his 12 from 12 wins. My program along with great techniques Billy has developed over the years with Australia’s top boxing coach Billy Hussein, the kid was just destined for continuous knockouts.

Is my style of training and program suitable for everyone?

In my opinion, Strength training and conditioning is a necessity to the majority of society. The daily struggles and challenges we face in our lives, from poor diet, lack of exercises, fatigue and lack of sleep, it all plays an impact. How many times have you heard this from your friends’ “he or she is so skinny, there so lucky and fit “ ! It’s not always the case! To ensure a long and healthy life, we need to start with our inner strength and ability to keep up with our busy schedules.

Kids:
Often I receive enquiries from parents asking me to train their kids to improve their fitness levels so coincide with their sports.
For example: Kids playing tennis, soccer, football etc., all need to increase their strength and agility to perform at their best.
Whether you’re an amateur, professional athlete or just someone who wants the best results with their fitness training, Maximum Fitness is the place to be.
